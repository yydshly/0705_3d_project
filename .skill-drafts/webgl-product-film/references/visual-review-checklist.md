# Visual Review Checklist

Use this before calling a 3D demo or film ready.

## Scene

- Does the first frame explain where the viewer is?
- Is there a clear hero object or visual subject?
- Are scale, grounding, contact shadows, and model proportions believable?
- Do materials match their role, such as soil looking like soil rather than gravel?
- Are clouds, fog, terrain, vegetation, and props consistent with the mood?

## Motion

- Does each camera move have a reason?
- Are cuts and transitions motivated by the story?
- Are there wide, medium, and close shots?
- Does the camera ever hold still enough for the viewer to understand?
- Is motion sickness avoided?

## Effects

- Are growth, wind, water, particles, or lighting changes timed believably?
- Are procedural effects irregular enough to feel natural?
- Is the final state stronger than the middle state?
- Are post-processing effects supportive rather than hiding defects?

## Publishing

- Is the video length appropriate for the intended platform?
- Are voiceover, subtitles, and visuals synchronized?
- Is the background music present but not competing with speech?
- Is the cover frame readable and visually representative?
- Is the output MP4 playable outside the development browser?
