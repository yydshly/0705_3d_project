# Product Direction

This research should eventually help us build our own product, not only understand other projects.

## Product Hypothesis

There is value in a workflow that turns 3D web capabilities into understandable, publishable, product-like experiences.

Current pain:

- Many WebGL demos are impressive but hard to understand.
- Many open-source 3D projects show capability but not product value.
- Turning a 3D demo into a good presentation requires technical, visual, director, audio, and publishing skills.
- People want immersive websites and 3D product displays, but the implementation path is scattered.

Our possible advantage:

- Combine code analysis, 3D direction, publishing workflow, and reusable Skill guidance.

## Candidate Product Directions

### 1. 3D Demo Analyzer

Input:

- GitHub repo or local project.

Output:

- Technical stack summary.
- Capability map.
- Rendering/shader/GPU explanation.
- Improvement suggestions.
- Demo/video plan.

Why it matters:

- Helps people understand unfamiliar 3D projects quickly.

### 2. 3D Product Film Generator

Input:

- Existing Three.js/WebGL project.
- Desired audience and platform.

Output:

- Cinematic demo timeline.
- Recording controls.
- Captions, voiceover, music.
- MP4, cover, publishing notes.

Why it matters:

- This is closest to what we already built with GrassSystemThreeJS.

### 3. Immersive Website Builder

Input:

- Product/portfolio content.
- 3D assets or generated visual concept.

Output:

- Scroll-driven 3D landing page.
- Product story sections.
- Mobile fallback.
- Optional share video.

Why it matters:

- Closest to Samsy Ninja-style output.
- High practical value for personal brands, products, portfolios, and launches.

### 4. 3D Product Viewer / Configurator

Input:

- GLB model, textures, product data.

Output:

- Inspectable model viewer.
- Hotspots and annotations.
- Lighting/environment presets.
- AR or embedded page.

Why it matters:

- More practical and commercial than pure creative demos.

### 5. Real-World Scene Showcase

Input:

- 3DGS splat, scan, or real scene asset.

Output:

- Browser-based spatial tour.
- Story points.
- Product/space annotations.
- Shareable video or webpage.

Why it matters:

- Future-facing direction for rooms, stores, exhibitions, real estate, travel, events, museums, and memory spaces.

## Recommended Path

Do not start with a full product platform.

Start with a research-backed prototype:

```text
Immersive 3D Product Story Page
```

Prototype scope:

- One product or personal-project story.
- Scroll-driven camera/object transitions.
- One hero 3D asset.
- Three to five story sections.
- Recordable product film mode.
- README and publishing package.

Why this is the best first product direction:

- It connects GrassSystemThreeJS learning with r3f-scroll-rig research.
- It is visible and easy to judge.
- It can produce both website and video output.
- It can become a reusable template later.

## Success Criteria For Our Own Product

- A non-technical viewer can understand the story in 60 seconds.
- The 3D effect supports the content instead of distracting from it.
- The page works as an interactive site and can also produce a shareable video.
- The implementation teaches us something reusable enough to upgrade the Skill.
